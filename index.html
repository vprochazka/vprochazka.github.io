Bio

Vladimir Prochazka is a PhD student at the Higgs Centre for Theoretical Physics working under Roman Zwicky. His research has been focused on understanding how anomalies can be used to constrain dynamics of QFT (in particular gauge theories). During his PhD Vladimir was working on obtaining explicit examples of the recently proven a-theorem. This theorem states that there exists a function equal to a specific coefficient in the 4D trace anomaly on curved space, which decreases under the RG flow and therefore counts the degrees of freedom along the RG flow. Knowledge of such function puts non-perturbative constraints on the low-energy physics. Another research direction is using the local coupling formalism to study contact terms in correlators of composite operators, which are intimately related to anomalies.

He is currently a visiting student at the Weizmann Intitute of Science working with Zohar Komargodski.
